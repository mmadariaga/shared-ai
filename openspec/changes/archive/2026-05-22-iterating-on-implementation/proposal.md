> **⚠ POST-HOC RECORD** — This proposal was backfilled after implementation. It describes a decision already made, not one being proposed.

## Why

When `/sai-3-implement` is re-run on a change that already has a partial `implementation.md`, the output regenerates every step in full — including steps that were already applied and committed. This inflates the context fed to `/sai-4-apply`, increasing token cost unnecessarily on each iteration.

## What Changes

- `sai/instructions/implement.md` — Added **Step 1b: Detect Already-Applied Steps** between the existing Step 1 (artifact loading) and Step 2 (ADR/DDR validation). The new step reads the existing `implementation.md`, identifies steps where every checkbox is `[x]`, and replaces their full content with a single `*(already applied)*` line in the regenerated output.
- `skills/claude/budget-executor/SKILL.md` — Renamed `name:` frontmatter field from `claude-budget-executor` to `budget-executor`.
- `skills/claude/budget-explorer/SKILL.md` — Renamed `name:` frontmatter field from `claude-budget-explorer` to `budget-explorer`.
- `skills/opencode/budget-executor/SKILL.md` — Renamed `name:` frontmatter field from `opencode-budget-executor` to `budget-executor`.
- `skills/opencode/budget-explorer/SKILL.md` — Renamed `name:` frontmatter field from `opencode-budget-explorer` to `budget-explorer`.

## Capabilities

### New Capabilities

- `implement-rerun-guard`: When `implementation.md` already exists, `/sai-3-implement` detects fully-applied steps (all checkboxes `[x]`) and emits only a `*(already applied)*` marker for them — reducing output size and apply-agent token cost on re-runs.

### Modified Capabilities

- `budget-skill-naming`: Budget skill `name:` fields normalized to platform-agnostic slugs (`budget-executor`, `budget-explorer`) in both Claude and OpenCode variants.
